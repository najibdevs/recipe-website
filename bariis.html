Bariis (Somali Rice)

Description

Somali rice, locally known as "Bariis," is a fragrant staple often seasoned with a mix of spices and sometimes adorned with raisins, vegetables, and meat.

Cooked to a fluffy consistency, it is typically served with rich stews or grilled meat, reflecting the culinary traditions of the Horn of Africa.

The harmonious blend of aromatic spices, such as cardamom and cumin, gives it a distinct and flavorful profile cherished in Somali households.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Water or broth
  • Cooking oil
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Spices
  • Carrots
  • Bell peppers
  • Salt
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice in cold water until clear, then drain.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a large pot over medium heat.
  3. Sauté chopped onion until translucent.
  4. Add minced garlic and sauté for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Stir in the spices (cardamom, cumin, cloves, and cinnamon stick) and cook until aromatic.
  6. Add the drained rice, ensuring it's coated with the oil and spices.
  7. Mix in carrots, bell peppers, and raisins if using.
  8. Pour in the water or broth and add salt.
  9. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low.
  10. Cover and simmer until rice is cooked and liquid is absorbed (about 15-20 minutes).
  11. Turn off heat, let sit for a few minutes, then fluff with a fork.
  12. Garnish with cilantro or parsley if desired.
  13. Serve hot with desired accompaniments.
